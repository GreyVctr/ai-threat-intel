## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: What is AI Shield Intelligence?**

A: It's a dedicated service that systematically collects, analyzes, and distributes intelligence about emerging threats to AI systems. We monitor academic research, security conferences, industry forums, code repositories, and public incidents to identify new attack methodologies before they impact your services.

**Q: Who is this program for?**

A: Organizations that deploy AI/ML systems in production and need to stay ahead of evolving AI-specific security threats. This includes security teams, AI/ML engineers, product security teams, and risk management professionals.

**Q: How is this different from traditional threat intelligence services?**

A: Traditional threat intelligence focuses on malware, network attacks, and infrastructure threats. We specialize exclusively in AI-specific threats like adversarial examples, model extraction, prompt injection, data poisoning, membership inference, and other attacks unique to AI systems. Our team has deep expertise in both AI/ML and security.

**Q: What types of threats do you cover?**

A: We cover all categories of AI security threats including:
- Adversarial attacks (evasion, poisoning, backdoors)
- Model extraction and stealing
- Privacy attacks (membership inference, model inversion)
- Prompt injection and jailbreaking for LLMs
- Training data poisoning
- Supply chain attacks on AI components
- Emerging research from academic and security communities

### Intelligence Collection

**Q: What sources do you monitor?**

A: We monitor 50+ academic sources (arXiv, conference proceedings, journals) and 30+ industry sources (security blogs, vendor advisories, threat sharing groups, GitHub repositories, security forums) daily. We also maintain direct relationships with researchers and participate in industry working groups.

**Q: How do you ensure intelligence quality and relevance?**

A: Our team of AI security experts reviews all findings, assesses severity and applicability, filters out noise, and provides context-specific analysis. We don't just forward raw data—we deliver actionable intelligence with clear recommendations.

**Q: How quickly do you detect new threats?**

A: We monitor sources continuously. Critical threats trigger real-time alerts within hours of public disclosure. Weekly briefings capture trends and lower-severity findings with deeper analysis.

**Q: Can we customize which sources you monitor for our organization?**

A: Yes. We can add organization-specific sources, adjust monitoring priorities, and focus on threats relevant to your particular AI use cases and technology stack.

### Intelligence Distribution

**Q: How do we receive threat intelligence?**

A: Through two channels:
1. Weekly briefings delivered via email/portal with comprehensive analysis of new threats, trends, and recommendations
2. Real-time alerts via email, Slack, PagerDuty, or other integrations for critical threats requiring immediate attention

**Q: What's included in the weekly briefings?**

A: Each briefing includes:
- New attack methodologies discovered that week
- Analysis of relevant security research and papers
- Public incidents involving AI systems
- Threat severity assessments and trend analysis
- Recommended defensive measures and mitigations
- Proof-of-concept tracking and weaponization timelines

**Q: What triggers a real-time alert?**

A: Critical threats that meet severity thresholds: actively exploited vulnerabilities, weaponized proof-of-concepts targeting common AI frameworks, zero-day disclosures affecting widely-used AI services, or threats specifically relevant to your technology stack.

**Q: Can we integrate alerts with our existing security tools?**

A: Yes. We support integrations with SIEM systems, ticketing platforms (Jira, ServiceNow), communication tools (Slack, Teams), and incident response platforms (PagerDuty, Splunk On-Call).
